PHYSICAL SOIL DEGRADATION


Physical soil degradation refers to the loss of soil's physical qualities, such as its structure, fertility, and water-holding capacity, due to natural or human-induced factors. This type of degradation can lead to reduced crop yields, decreased water quality, and increased greenhouse gas emissions.


causes of physical soil degradation


 - Intensive tillage: Repeated use of heavy machinery and tillage practices can damage soil structure, reduce organic matter, and increase erosion ยน.
        - Soil erosion: Water or wind erosion can remove topsoil, reducing soil fertility and structure.
        - Compaction: Heavy machinery and foot traffic can compact soil, reducing its water-holding capacity and aeration.
        - Flooding and landslides: Natural disasters can damage soil structure and fertility.

Effects of soil degradation

- Reduced crop yields: Degraded soil can lead to reduced crop yields and lower agricultural productivity.
    - Decreased water quality: Soil erosion and degradation can lead to increased sedimentation and nutrient pollution in waterways.
    - Increased greenhouse gas emissions: Degraded soil can release stored carbon into the atmosphere, contributing to climate change.

Prevention of physical soil degradation

- Conservation tillage: Reduce or eliminate intensive tillage practices to preserve soil structure and organic matter. - Crop rotation and cover cropping: Plant cover crops and rotate crops to improve soil fertility and structure. - Terracing and contour farming: Implement terracing and contour farming practices to reduce soil erosion and improve water retention. - Soil testing and monitoring: Regularly test and monitor soil health to identify areas for improvement.i>
